ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE IN HEALTHCARE ETHICS

Ethical Considerations

Here are some key ethical considerations related to the use of artificial intelligence (AI) in healthcare:

1. Bias and Fairness: AI systems must be trained and tested on diverse datasets that accurately represent the population. Special attention should be given to identifying and mitigating biases that could lead to discriminatory outcomes, particularly regarding race, gender, or socioeconomic factors.

2. Informed Consent: Patients should be well-informed about the use of AI in their healthcare and have the right to understand and consent to its implementation. Clear communication is necessary to ensure patients understand the potential risks and benefits of AI-based interventions.

3. Human Autonomy and Decision-Making: While AI can support clinical decision-making, the ultimate responsibility should remain with human healthcare providers. The role of AI should be viewed as augmenting human judgment rather than replacing it, respecting the autonomy and expertise of healthcare professionals.
